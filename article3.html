Should You Repair or Replace Your Furnace?

When your furnace starts acting up, it can be difficult to know whether a repair will solve the problem or if it’s time for a full replacement. Making the right choice can save you money and improve your home’s comfort.

1. How Old Is Your Furnace?

Most furnaces last between 12–18 years. If your system is over 12 years old and experiencing issues, replacement may be more cost‑effective.

2. How Much Will the Repair Cost?

A good rule of thumb: If the repair costs more than 30% of a new furnace, replacement is usually the better option.

3. Are You Experiencing Frequent Breakdowns?

If your furnace needs repairs every winter, it may be nearing the end of its lifespan. Replacing it can save you money long‑term.

4. Are Your Energy Bills Increasing?

Older furnaces lose efficiency over time. A new high‑efficiency system can significantly reduce your heating costs.

5. Is Your Home Heating Uneven?

Uneven heating often indicates airflow issues, failing components, or an undersized system. Replacement may solve these problems permanently.

Repair vs Replace: Quick Guide

  • Repair: Furnace under 10 years old, minor issues, low repair cost.
  • Replace: Furnace over 12 years old, expensive repairs, frequent breakdowns.
  • Upgrade: If you want better efficiency, quieter operation, or lower bills.
