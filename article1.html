How to Know When Your AC Needs Repair

Your air conditioner is one of the most important systems in your home during the summer. When it starts acting up, it’s important to recognize the signs early so you can avoid expensive breakdowns.

1. Your AC Is Blowing Warm Air

If your AC is running but the air isn’t cold, you may have a refrigerant leak, compressor failure, or blocked coils.

2. Weak or No Airflow

Poor airflow often means a clogged filter, blower motor issue, or duct blockage. If airflow suddenly drops, shut the system off and schedule service.

3. Strange Noises

Grinding, rattling, buzzing, or clicking noises usually indicate mechanical problems. These issues can worsen quickly if ignored.

4. Water Leaks Around the Unit

Water pooling near your AC often means a clogged drain line or frozen evaporator coil.

5. Higher Energy Bills

If your AC is suddenly costing more to run, it may be struggling due to dirty coils, low refrigerant, or worn components.

When to Call a Professional

If you notice any of these signs, it’s best to schedule an AC inspection. Early repairs prevent major damage and keep your home cool and comfortable.
